STEP 1 ‚Äî Find an English-Taught Programme in Italy

A complete, simple guide for Ethiopian beginners

If you are in Ethiopia and dreaming of studying in Italy, your very first step is to find the right programme.

Before you think about visas, pre-enrolment, or scholarships, you must know:

  • What exactly you want to study
  • Which university offers it
  • In which city
  • In English (not Italian!)

This guide walks you slowly, step by step, assuming you have never used Universitaly before and you are completely new to the Italian system.

üîπ 1. Get Clear on Your Academic Profile

First, you need to understand who you are as a student. This will save you months of confusion and help you avoid programmes where you don't meet the requirements.

1.1 Choose your level of study

üìò Bachelor's Degree (Laurea Triennale ‚Äì 3 years)

For students who have (or will soon have) Ethiopian Grade 12 / high school diploma.

üìó Master's Degree (Laurea Magistrale ‚Äì 2 years)

For students who already have a Bachelor's degree (for example, from an Ethiopian university).

üìï Single-Cycle Degree (5‚Äì6 years)

Long programmes that combine Bachelor + Master in one, such as:

  • Medicine and Surgery
  • Dentistry
  • Architecture
  • Law

1.2 Decide your field of interest

Think honestly about what you enjoy and where you see your future. Some common options:

  • Computer Science / Data Science / AI
  • Engineering (Civil, Mechanical, Electrical, Computer, etc.)
  • Economics, Business & Management
  • Medicine / Health Sciences / Nursing (check carefully for English-taught)
  • Architecture, Design & Fine Arts
  • Social Sciences (Political Science, Sociology, International Relations)
  • Humanities (Languages, History, Philosophy, Cultural Studies)

1.3 Language of instruction

For this guide and your website, we focus on English-taught programmes only.

üëâ

So your choice is: Programmes taught fully in English.
(If a programme is "mainly Italian" with a few English courses, do not choose it.)

1.4 Study mode

Most international students choose:

  • On-campus (in presence) ‚Äì you live and study in Italy.

Other modes (online, blended) are rare and usually not suitable for student visa purposes.

1.5 Starting period

In Italy, the academic year typically runs from September to the following September.
Applications usually open between November and April (exact dates depend on each university).

üéØ Outcome of Step 1.1‚Äì1.5

By now, you should be able to write a simple, clear sentence like:

"I want a 2-year Master's in Economics, taught fully in English, on-campus in Italy, starting in September."

If you can say this, you're ready for the next part.

üîπ 2. Understand the Italian University System (Quick & Simple)

You don't need to become an expert, but a basic understanding will help a lot.

2.1 Types of institutions

In Italy, higher education includes:

  • Universities (public and private)
  • Polytechnic Universities ‚Äì mainly Engineering, Architecture, Design
  • Fine Arts & Design Academies
  • Music & Performing Arts Academies
  • Specialized schools and institutes

The official national portal Universitaly is managed under the Italian Ministry of University and Research and is the main reference point for programmes and pre-enrolment for international students.

2.2 Degree structure

  • Bachelor's (Laurea Triennale) ‚Äì 3 years, 1st cycle
  • Master's (Laurea Magistrale) ‚Äì 2 years, 2nd cycle
  • Single-cycle ‚Äì 5 or 6 years (Medicine, Dentistry, Law, some Architecture programmes)

2.3 For Ethiopian students specifically

  • To apply for a Bachelor's, you normally need a secondary school qualification equivalent to at least 12 years of schooling (Ethiopian Grade 12 ‚Äì plus sometimes extra requirements depending on the programme).
  • To apply for a Master's, you need a Bachelor's degree in a related field.
  • For both, universities will check your academic background, grades, and subject match with the programme.

üéØ Outcome of Step 2

You understand:

  • Which degree level you can apply for
  • What kind of Italian institution you will likely join

Now we move to the practical part: searching for actual English-taught programmes.

üîπ 4. Use "Where to Study" to Understand Cities & Universities

After you've explored what to study, you must understand where you will live and study.

Universitaly has a "Where to Study" section that lists Italian institutions by region and city.

4.1 Explore institutions by region

  1. Go to the "Where to Study" page on Universitaly (search for "cerca istituzioni" or use the "Where to Study" menu).
  2. Select a region (e.g., Lombardy, Lazio, Veneto, Emilia-Romagna, Tuscany).
  3. See which universities, academies, and other institutions are located there.

4.2 Connect your programme list to real cities

Now compare:

  • Your programme list from Step 3
  • The universities and locations you see under "Where to Study"

Ask yourself:

  • Do I prefer a large city (Milan, Rome, Turin) or a smaller, quieter city (Parma, Trento, Siena)?
  • What about climate? (Northern Italy can be cold, southern warmer.)
  • How might cost of living differ between cities (rent, transport, food)?
  • Does the university seem to have support for international students (English website, international office, orientation guides)?

Make simple notes like:

"University of Padua ‚Äì medium-size city, strong in science, many international programmes."
"University of Milan ‚Äì big city, higher cost of living, strong reputation, many English programmes."

üîπ 5. Open Each University's Official Website

5.1 Confirm the language of instruction

On the programme page, look for:

  • Check if the entire programme or only some courses are in English.
    If it is not clearly fully in English, treat it carefully.

5.2 Check entry requirements

Look for sections like "Admission Requirements" or "How to Apply". Common requirements include:

  • Required degree (e.g., a Bachelor's in a related field for a Master's programme)
  • Specific background subjects (e.g., maths/physics for engineering)
  • Possible minimum grade or GPA
  • For some fields (Medicine, some Engineering, etc.), admission tests (e.g., IMAT, TOLC etc.)

5.3 Check English language requirements

Different universities have different rules:

  • Some accept no test if your previous degree was taught in English or if you studied in an English-medium school (check if this applies to your Ethiopian school/university).
  • Others require a language certificate, such as:
    • IELTS
    • TOEFL
    • Cambridge English
    • Duolingo English Test or similar (varies by university)

Carefully read:

  • Required minimum level (often B2)
  • Whether they accept letters from your university stating "medium of instruction is English".

5.4 Check required documents

Most universities will list documents like:

  • Passport
  • High school diploma (for Bachelor) or Bachelor's degree (for Master)
  • Official academic transcripts
  • Motivation / statement of purpose
  • CV (Curriculum Vitae)
  • Recommendation letters
  • Proof of English proficiency (if required)

Write down exactly what each programme asks for so you can prepare later.

STEP 2 ‚Äî Verify Programme Entry Requirements

  • Qualification mismatch
  • Missing subjects
  • Wrong degree level

  • Recognition procedures you weren't prepared for

  3. Note the minimum required level.

    • Universities may set different standards even for similar programmes ‚Äî some more flexible, some more competitive.

üéØ Outcome

‚úî What you must do

  2. Identify whether education in English qualifies for exemption.

1Ô∏è‚É£ You Got Accepted ‚Äì What Happens Next?

Once an Italian university accepts you (sometimes "conditionally accepted"), that does NOT mean you can just take your passport and fly.

2Ô∏è‚É£ What Is the Universitaly "Summary"?

üîç In simple words

The Universitaly pre-enrolment summary is a PDF document generated by the Universitaly portal after you complete your pre-enrolment and the university validates it.

  • Confirms which university and programme you are pre-enrolling in
  • Shows your personal data
  • Confirms the embassy/consulate where you'll apply for a visa
  • Is used as official proof when you go to the Italian Embassy / VFS

Universities and official guides clearly say that, once validated, you must download and print this summary and present it to the Italian Embassy/Consulate in your country.

üßæ

Think of it like this:

The summary = an official bridge between your university acceptance and the Embassy.

It tells the Embassy: "This person has been pre-enrolled in this Italian university and wants a study visa."

4Ô∏è‚É£ After You Get the Summary ‚Äì Why Legalisation & Recognition Are Needed

Now you have your university acceptance and Universitaly summary. The next major question is:

"How does Italy officially recognise my Ethiopian school or university qualifications?"

For this, Italian institutions (universities + Embassy) use recognition tools:

  • CIMEA statements (Comparability / Verification), AND/OR
  • Declaration of Value (DoV) issued by the Italian Embassy

For an Ethiopian student, the Embassy of Italy in Addis Ababa plays a central role.

5Ô∏è‚É£ What Is the Declaration of Value (DoV)?

The Declaration of Value (Dichiarazione di Valore ‚Äì DoV) is:

  • An official document written in Italian,
  • Issued by the Italian Embassy/Consulate,
  • Describing the nature, level, and value of your foreign qualification in its own country (Ethiopia).

It does not automatically make your degree "equivalent", but it is necessary for:

  • Applying to Italian universities
  • Having your studies recognised
  • Visa procedures for study purposes
‚ö†Ô∏è

For Ethiopia, the Embassy clearly states that the DoV is issued for foreign qualifications (high school or degree) and requires your documents to be legalized and translated into Italian first.

6Ô∏è‚É£ Which Documents Must Be Legalised for Ethiopian Students?

From the Embassy of Italy in Addis Ababa, for the academic year 2025/26:

üßë‚Äçüéì A. If you are a Diploma holder (high school / university entrance)

  • Pre-evaluation / Acceptance letter from the Italian university

8Ô∏è‚É£ CIMEA: The Alternative (or Complement) to DoV

üîç What is CIMEA?

CIMEA is Italy's official ENIC-NARIC centre for the recognition of foreign qualifications.

They issue two key documents:

Statement of Comparability

Explains how your foreign qualification compares to the Italian system (level, type, access rights).

Statement of Verification

Confirms the authenticity of your qualification.

  4. Pay the fees.
  5. Wait for evaluation and issuance of the Statements (digital PDF).

  • Some still require documents to be legalised / apostilled and a CIMEA statement.

üîë Always follow exactly what your university asks for:

  • If they say "CIMEA OR DoV" ‚Üí choose the easier/faster option for you
  • If they say "CIMEA AND legalisation" ‚Üí you must do both
